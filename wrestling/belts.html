The Belts



Here is a breakdown of the significance of the 5 major belts in WWE Championship:



WWE World Heavyweight Championship: This prestigious title has seen various designs over the years. The classic “Big Gold” belt, adorned with eagle motifs, symbolized the pinnacle of wrestling achievement. More recently, the WWE Championship features a sleeker design, often referred to as the “Winged Eagle” or “Spinner” belt.



Intercontinental Championship: Known for its white leather strap and iconic oval-shaped center plate, the Intercontinental Championship has been held by legendary wrestlers like Shawn Michaels, Bret Hart, and The Rock. It represents excellence in the mid-card division.



United States Championship: The U.S. title boasts a red, white, and blue color scheme. Wrestlers like John Cena and Ric Flair have proudly worn this belt, which signifies national pride and competition.



Women’s Championships: WWE has separate titles for women, including the RAW Women’s Championship and the SmackDown Women’s Championship. These belts have evolved to reflect the women’s revolution in professional wrestling.



Tag Team Championships: The tag team division features titles like the RAW Tag Team Championship and the SmackDown Tag Team Championship. These belts emphasize teamwork and collaboration among wrestlers.
